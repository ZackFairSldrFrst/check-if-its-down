Why Cloudflare Outages Matter

Critical Infrastructure

Cloudflare isn't just another tech company—it's a fundamental piece of internet infrastructure. The company processes over 50 million HTTP requests per second on average and protects millions of websites from cyber attacks. When Cloudflare goes down, a significant portion of the internet effectively becomes inaccessible.

Single Point of Failure

The November 18, 2025 outage highlighted the risks of internet infrastructure centralization. Despite the internet being designed with redundancy in mind, the practical reality is that a small number of companies—including Cloudflare, Amazon Web Services, and Google Cloud—control a disproportionate amount of internet traffic.

Economic Impact

A Cloudflare outage doesn't just affect user experience—it has real economic consequences:

  • E-commerce losses: Online stores lose sales every minute they're offline
  • Advertising revenue: Platforms lose ad impression opportunities
  • Productivity: Businesses can't access critical online tools
  • Reputation damage: Users lose confidence in affected services

Common Causes of Cloudflare Outages

Traffic Spikes

As reported in the November 18 incident, unusual traffic spikes can overwhelm even Cloudflare's massive infrastructure. These spikes could be caused by DDoS attacks, configuration errors, or sudden viral content.

Software Updates

Like any technology company, Cloudflare regularly updates its systems. Occasionally, these updates can introduce bugs or unexpected behavior that affects service availability.

BGP Routing Issues

Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) problems can cause widespread internet outages. If Cloudflare's routing becomes misconfigured, traffic may be unable to reach its destination.

Cyber Attacks

While Cloudflare specializes in protecting others from cyber attacks, the company itself can become a target. Sophisticated attacks targeting Cloudflare's infrastructure could potentially cause service disruptions.

How to Check If Cloudflare Is Down

1. Check This Status Page: We monitor Cloudflare's network status in real-time and provide immediate updates on any detected issues.

2. Official Status Page: Visit status.cloudflare.com for official updates directly from Cloudflare (note: this page may also be affected during major outages).

3. Look for Patterns: If multiple unrelated websites are all down simultaneously, it's likely a Cloudflare issue rather than problems with individual sites.

4. Check Social Media: During outages, users typically report issues on platforms like Reddit and any social media sites that remain operational.

What to Do During a Cloudflare Outage

Be Patient: Cloudflare has world-class engineering teams who work quickly to resolve issues. Most outages are resolved within a few hours.

Don't Flood Refresh: Repeatedly refreshing affected pages won't help and may actually slow down recovery efforts.

Check Back Here: We provide real-time updates on Cloudflare's status and estimated recovery times.

Understand the Scope: Remember that Cloudflare outages affect many websites simultaneously. The problem isn't with your internet connection or device.
