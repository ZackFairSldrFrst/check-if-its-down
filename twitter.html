What Causes Twitter/X Outages?

Infrastructure Dependencies

Twitter relies on third-party infrastructure providers like Cloudflare for content delivery and DDoS protection. When these providers experience issues, Twitter can become inaccessible even if Twitter's own servers are functioning normally.

Common Outage Causes

Third-Party CDN Issues: As demonstrated by the November 18, 2025 incident, problems with content delivery networks can knock Twitter offline for millions of users simultaneously.

Internal Technical Issues: Software bugs, database problems, or server failures can cause partial or complete outages.

Overwhelming Traffic: Major breaking news events can cause traffic spikes that strain Twitter's infrastructure.

DDoS Attacks: Distributed denial of service attacks targeting Twitter or its infrastructure providers can make the platform temporarily unavailable.

What to Do During a Twitter Outage

Be Patient: Most outages are resolved within a few hours. Twitter's engineering team works quickly to restore service.

Don't Panic: Outages are typically temporary. Your account, tweets, and data remain safe during downtime.
